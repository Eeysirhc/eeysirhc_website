Cryptocurrencies rely on distributed public ledger technology where you need a network of computers (in this case miners) to validate all those transactions. Different cryptocurrencies use different types of consensus algorithms to carry out that validation work by solving complex mathematical puzzles. This ensures the integrity of all past, present, and future records on the blockchain.

Bitcoin, for example, uses a popular consensus algorithm called Proof of Work (PoW) which leverages the raw processing power from your computer hardware to complete these computations. Other notable coins include Ethereum and Dogecoin.

In the last few years there have been an explosion of new consensus algorithms to move to greener and more efficient mining processes. Below are a few of them with some examples.

  • Proof of Stake (PoS): you “mine” coins based on the amount of crypto that you purchase and hold
    • Cardano and Ethereum 2.0 (ETH is currently PoW but moving to PoS soon)
  • Proof of Capacity (PoC): you “mine” coins based on the amount of hard drive space you provide to the network
    • Filecoin, Storj
  • Proof of Spacetime (PoST): you “mine” coins based on the amount of hard drive space you provide to the network AND how long it has been actively running
    • Chia, Spacemesh
  • Proof of Coverage: you “mine” coins by providing bandwidth to the network
    • Helium Network uses wifi/radio frequency for coverage
    • Mysterium Network relies on your IP address for usage
    • PlanetWatch is an IoT device where you record the air quality in your region
  • Proof of Authority (PoA): you “mine” coins by having your identify validated
    • VeChain
